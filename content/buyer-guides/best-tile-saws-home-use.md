## Buyer's Guide: Choosing a Tile Saw for Home Projects

### Blade Size
- **7-inch blade:** Handles tiles up to 12×12 inches — good for standard home projects
- **10-inch blade:** Handles tiles up to 24×24 inches — needed for large-format or natural stone

### Motor Power (HP/Amps)
- **3/5 HP – 1 HP:** Ceramic and light porcelain, standard home projects
- **1.5–2.0 HP:** Porcelain, travertine, marble, large tiles

### Tile Types by Difficulty
| Tile Type | Difficulty | Recommended Power |
|---|---|---|
| Ceramic | Easy | Any model |
| Glazed porcelain | Medium | 1.0+ HP |
| Through-body porcelain | Hard | 1.5+ HP |
| Natural stone (marble, travertine) | Medium-Hard | 1.0+ HP |
| Granite | Very Hard | 1.5+ HP |

### Wet vs Dry Cutting
Always use a wet saw for tile. Dry cutting creates silica dust (hazardous) and overheats the blade. Wet saws are mandatory for any significant tile project.

## Tips for Cutting Tile at Home

1. **Mark your cut line with a pencil or china marker** — visible and easy to follow
2. **Let the saw do the work** — gentle forward pressure only; forcing causes chipping
3. **Support the tile on both sides of the cut** — especially for long thin strips
4. **Use a diamond blade** — standard grit blades dull fast on porcelain
5. **Change the water regularly** — cloudy water with debris reduces cut quality

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I cut large porcelain tiles with a 7-inch saw?**
Only if they're 12×12 or smaller. Large-format porcelain (18×18+) requires a 10-inch saw with sufficient rip capacity.

**Do I need a tile saw for a small bathroom?**
For a standard 60 sq ft bathroom with standard 12×12 tiles, a 7-inch budget saw is perfectly adequate. Rent one if you prefer not to buy.

**Can a tile saw cut glass tile?**
Yes, with a fine-grit diamond blade. Glass tiles chip easily — use a slow feed rate and a blade specifically rated for glass.

**How long do diamond blades last?**
For home use, a quality 7-inch diamond blade lasts 60–120 linear feet of cutting. A 10-inch blade lasts longer. Replace when cuts become ragged or the blade wanders.
