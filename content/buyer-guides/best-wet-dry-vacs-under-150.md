## Buyer Guide: What to Look For in a Wet/Dry Vac Under $150

### Capacity: Match It to Your Tasks
A 5-6 gallon vac handles most household and small workshop tasks. If you frequently clean up large messes or want to go longer between empties, opt for a 10-16 gallon model like the Craftsman CMXEVBE17595. Remember that larger tanks are heavier and take up more storage space.

### Suction Power and CFM
Peak HP is the most commonly advertised spec, but CFM (cubic feet per minute) is more useful for comparing real-world performance. For wet/dry vacs in this price range, look for at least 60-80 CFM at the hose end. Higher CFM means faster pickup of debris and better airflow through partially clogged filters.

### Wet Pickup: Do Not Skip the Foam Filter
For wet pickup, always use a foam filter sleeve rather than a paper or fabric cartridge filter. Using a dry filter with liquids can damage the motor and reduce filter life. The Ridgid WD0671, Shop-Vac 5986000, and Stanley SL18116P all include foam filters for wet use.

### Accessories and Accessories Storage
Check what accessories come in the box — a longer hose, floor nozzle, crevice tool, and brush are the most useful. Onboard storage for these accessories (like the DEWALT DXV09P offers) keeps them from getting lost and makes the vac more convenient to grab and go.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What is the difference between a shop vac and a wet/dry vac?**
The terms are used interchangeably — both refer to vacuums designed to pick up both dry debris and liquids. Traditional household vacuums are not designed for liquid pickup and can be damaged by it.

**Can I vacuum drywall dust with a wet/dry vac?**
Yes, but fine drywall dust can quickly clog standard filters. Use a fine-dust cartridge filter or HEPA filter for drywall cleanup, and consider a dust separator attachment to extend filter life. Standard cartridge filters can be overwhelmed by fine particles.

**How do I clean a wet/dry vac filter?**
Remove the filter and tap it gently against a trash can to dislodge debris. For cartridge filters, rinse with water and allow to dry completely before reinstalling. Never use a wet filter — it significantly reduces suction and can damage the motor.

**Do wet/dry vacs have good suction for water?**
Yes — most wet/dry vacs are very effective at picking up standing water. For large flood situations, look for a model with a bottom drain port (like the Ridgid WD0671) for faster emptying without tipping the tank.
