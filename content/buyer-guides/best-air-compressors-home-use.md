## Buyer Guide: What to Look For in a Home Air Compressor

### Tank Size and Portability
For most home DIYers, a 6-8 gallon tank strikes the right balance. Smaller 1-2 gallon "hot dog" tanks are highly portable but require constant cycling. Larger tanks (20+ gallons) hold more air but are stationary. Pancake-style tanks are the most stable and portable for garage use.

### CFM: The Most Important Spec
CFM (cubic feet per minute) determines which tools your compressor can run. Most brad nailers require 0.5-1.0 SCFM, while spray guns need 4-8 SCFM or more. Always choose a compressor with more CFM than your most demanding tool requires. The Makita MAC2400 at 4.2 SCFM handles the widest range of tools.

### Oil-Free vs. Oil-Lubricated
Oil-free pumps are maintenance-free and better for occasional use — ideal for most homeowners. Oil-lubricated pumps run quieter, cooler, and last longer under heavy use, but require periodic oil changes. For heavy workshop use, oil-lubricated models like the Makita MAC2400 are worth the extra maintenance.

### Noise Level
Compressors range from about 60 dB (California Air Tools 8010) to 90+ dB for industrial models. If you work in an attached garage or near living areas, a quiet model is worth the premium. At 79 dB, the Makita MAC2400 is the loudest on this list but still manageable with hearing protection.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What size air compressor do I need for home use?**
A 6-8 gallon pancake or hotdog compressor handles 90% of home DIY tasks — inflation, nailers, and light spray work. If you plan to use an impact wrench or spray gun regularly, consider a larger 10-20 gallon model with higher CFM output.

**Can I use an air compressor to fill car tires?**
Yes — any compressor with a tire inflation attachment can fill car tires. You only need about 35-40 PSI for most passenger cars, so even a small 6-gallon unit can do the job quickly.

**How often should I drain the air compressor tank?**
After every use, or at minimum once a week if used regularly. Moisture accumulates in the tank and can cause rust over time. Most tanks have a drain valve at the bottom — simply open it briefly to release the condensate.

**What maintenance does an air compressor need?**
Oil-free models need very little — just clean the air intake filter occasionally. Oil-lubricated models require oil level checks before each use and oil changes every 3-6 months or per the manufacturer schedule.
