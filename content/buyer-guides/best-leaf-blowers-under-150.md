## Buyer's Guide: CFM vs MPH — What Actually Matters?

### CFM (Cubic Feet per Minute)
CFM measures the volume of air moved. More CFM = better at moving large piles of leaves. This is the more important number for yard work.

### MPH (Miles per Hour)
MPH measures air velocity. High MPH helps blast stubborn debris off wet surfaces.

### For best performance: you want both
- **300+ CFM + 90+ MPH** = adequate for most yards
- **400+ CFM + 100+ MPH** = excellent for large properties

### Battery vs Corded vs Gas

| Type | Pro | Con |
|---|---|---|
| Battery | Cordless, quiet, no fumes | Limited runtime |
| Corded | Unlimited runtime, low cost | Extension cord limitation |
| Gas | Maximum power, long runtime | Loud, maintenance required, fumes |

For most homeowners under $150, **battery** is the best choice.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How many CFM do I need for my yard?**
- Small yard/patio (under 1/4 acre): 150–250 CFM
- Medium yard (1/4–1/2 acre): 250–400 CFM
- Large yard (1/2+ acre): 400+ CFM or consider a backpack blower

**Are cordless leaf blowers as good as gas?**
Modern 40V and 56V cordless blowers match gas blowers under 25cc in practical performance. Gas remains better for very large properties (1+ acre) where runtime is critical.

**How long do cordless leaf blower batteries last?**
Most 2.0–2.5Ah batteries last 25–45 minutes of continuous use. Variable speed helps extend runtime.

**Can I use a leaf blower to clear snow?**
Yes, for light/fresh snow (under 2 inches). A high-CFM blower clears powder snow from driveways quickly. Not effective for wet or packed snow.
