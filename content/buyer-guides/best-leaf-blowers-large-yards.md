## Buyer Guide: What to Look For in a Leaf Blower for Large Yards

### CFM vs. MPH: Which Matters More?
CFM (cubic feet per minute) measures how much air volume the blower moves — this is the key spec for clearing large areas quickly. MPH measures the velocity of the air — important for dislodging stubborn, wet leaves. For large yards, prioritize CFM first. Aim for at least 400 CFM to clear a half-acre property in a reasonable time.

### Battery vs. Gas for Large Yards
Gas models offer unlimited runtime and more raw power, making them the traditional choice for large properties. Modern battery blowers have closed the gap significantly — the EGO LB6504 at 650 CFM and the DEWALT DCBL772X1 at 600 CFM rival entry-level gas models. For most homeowners, a high-capacity cordless blower with a spare battery is now a viable alternative to gas.

### Handheld vs. Backpack Design
Handheld blowers are lighter and easier to store but can fatigue your arm during long sessions. Backpack blowers distribute weight across your shoulders and back — much more comfortable for 30+ minute sessions on a large yard. If you regularly spend more than 20 minutes blowing leaves, a backpack model like the Husqvarna 350BT is worth considering.

### Noise Levels
Gas blowers typically run at 90-105 dB — always wear hearing protection. Cordless blowers are notably quieter, usually 65-75 dB. If noise is a concern for neighbors or early morning use, cordless models like the EGO LB6504 and Greenworks Pro 80V are significantly more considerate.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How many CFM do I need for a large yard?**
For a quarter-acre property, 350-400 CFM is sufficient. For half an acre or more, look for 450+ CFM. The models on this list (all 412-650 CFM) are well-suited for large yard clearing.

**What is the best leaf blower for wet leaves?**
Wet leaves are dense and heavy — they require high MPH to dislodge and high CFM to move. The EGO LB6504 (650 CFM, 180 MPH) and Husqvarna 350BT (494 CFM, 180 MPH) are the strongest choices for wet-leaf conditions.

**Should I buy a leaf blower with a vacuum function?**
Vacuum/mulcher combos are useful for small areas and garden beds but slow you down significantly on large properties. For large yards, a dedicated high-CFM blower is much faster than any vacuum combo. Blow leaves to a collection area, then use a rake or tarp to bag them.

**How do I maintain a gas leaf blower?**
Use fresh fuel (no more than 30 days old), clean or replace the air filter annually, and check/replace the spark plug each season. Use ethanol-free fuel when possible to prevent carburetor issues. Store with the fuel tank emptied or treated with stabilizer over winter.
