## Frequently Asked Questions

**What is the best circular saw blade for plywood?**
Use a 40-tooth or 60-tooth fine-finish blade. High tooth counts minimize tear-out on veneered plywood. A standard 24-tooth framing blade will leave rough edges.

**Can a circular saw cut hardwood?**
Yes, with the right blade. A 40+ tooth blade handles hardwood. Feed slowly and let the saw do the work — forcing causes binding.

**How deep can a 7-1/4 inch circular saw cut?**
Typically 2-1/2 inches at 90°, 1-3/4 inches at 45°. Sufficient for all standard dimensional lumber (2×4, 2×6, 4×4 with two passes).

**Do I need a circular saw if I have a miter saw?**
Yes — circular saws are better for breaking down large sheet goods (plywood, OSB) and cutting lumber to rough length. Miter saws excel at precise angle cuts on narrower boards.
