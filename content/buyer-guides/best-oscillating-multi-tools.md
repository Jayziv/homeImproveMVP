## What Can an Oscillating Multi-Tool Do?

### Cutting

- **Plunge cuts:** Cut access holes in drywall, floors, or tile backer

### Scraping

## Buyer's Guide: Oscillating Tool Basics

### OPM (Oscillations Per Minute)
- 10,000–12,000 OPM: Fine control, finish work
- 15,000–20,000 OPM: Fast cutting

### Oscillation Angle
- 2.8°–3.2°: Wider angle = faster cutting, more vibration
- Narrow angle = more precise, less vibration

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can an oscillating tool cut through nails?**
Yes — use a bi-metal blade. These cut through nails, screws, and even rebar. Flush-cutting nails in flooring is one of the most popular uses.

**What is the best oscillating tool blade for cutting wood?**
A high-tooth bi-metal blade for clean cuts, or a carbide-grit blade for fast rough cuts. For precision, use a fine-tooth blade with 14–18 TPI.

**Can an oscillating tool remove grout?**
Yes — it's one of the best tools for grout removal. Use a carbide grout removal blade. Work slowly to avoid chipping adjacent tiles.

**How do I reduce vibration when using an oscillating tool?**
Use variable speed — lower OPM means less vibration. Fein's tools have the best vibration damping. Wear gloves for extended use.
