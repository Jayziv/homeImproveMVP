## Buyer's Guide: Airless vs HVLP for Fences

### Airless Sprayers
- **Best for:** Large areas, thick paints, fast coverage
- **Coverage:** 200–500 sq ft/hr
- **Overspray:** High — always mask surrounding areas and plants
- **Best paint types:** Latex, oil-based, stains, sealers

### HVLP Sprayers
- **Best for:** Smooth finishes, smaller areas, decorative work
- **Coverage:** Slower than airless
- **Overspray:** Very low — minimal masking needed
- **Best paint types:** Thinned latex, stains, lacquers

## Tips for Spraying a Fence

1. **Cover plants and ground:** Lay drop cloths and cover all shrubs near the fence
2. **Choose the right day:** Ideal conditions are 50–85°F, low humidity, no wind
3. **Thin paint if needed:** HVLP sprayers generally need paint thinned 10–15% with water (latex) or mineral spirits (oil-based)
4. **Maintain consistent distance:** Keep the sprayer 10–12 inches from the surface for even coverage
5. **Overlap passes by 50%:** Overlap each spray pass by half its width to avoid stripes
6. **Two thin coats > one thick coat:** Let the first coat dry before applying the second

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I use a regular paint sprayer on a fence?**
Yes, any of the models above work on fences. Airless sprayers are fastest for large sections; HVLP gives a finer finish for decorative work.

**What is the best paint for wooden fences?**
Exterior latex or acrylic paint works well. For natural wood, a penetrating stain provides better long-term protection than paint. Solid stains are the best of both worlds.

**How much fence can I spray per hour?**
An airless sprayer like the Graco X5 can cover 200–400 linear feet per hour. An HVLP sprayer is roughly half that speed.

**Do I need to prime a fence before spraying?**
New bare wood should be primed. Previously painted fences may only need a light coat of primer on bare spots. Always clean and dry the fence first.
