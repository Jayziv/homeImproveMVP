## Buyer Guide: What to Look For in a Circular Saw

### Corded vs. Cordless
Corded circular saws deliver unlimited runtime and consistent power regardless of battery charge. They are ideal for long cutting sessions and heavy-duty tasks. Cordless saws offer portability and are perfect for job sites, decks, and anywhere without easy outlet access. For most homeowners, a cordless 6-1/2 inch saw handles 90% of tasks.

### Blade Size: 6-1/2 in. vs. 7-1/4 in.
7-1/4 inch blades are the standard for full-size saws — they cut deeper (up to 2-1/2 in. at 90 degrees) and accept a wider range of specialty blades. 6-1/2 inch blades are lighter and more common in cordless saws. For most homeowner tasks like cutting 2x4s and 3/4 in. plywood, either blade size is sufficient.

### Bevel Capacity
Most saws bevel from 0 to 45 degrees — enough for basic miter cuts and angled work. Some models go to 50 or 56 degrees, giving you more flexibility for compound cuts. Look for a smooth, positive-stop bevel adjustment with a clear angle scale.

### Safety Features
An electric brake that stops the blade quickly after you release the trigger is an important safety feature — the Makita 5007MGA and Milwaukee 2730-20 both include it. Also look for a blade guard that retracts smoothly and reliably.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What circular saw blade should I use for plywood?**
For plywood, use a 40-tooth or higher finish blade. More teeth produce cleaner cuts with less splintering. For rough framing cuts in 2x lumber, a 24-tooth blade cuts faster and is fine for construction-grade material.

**Can a circular saw cut metal?**
Yes, with the right blade — a metal-cutting abrasive disc or carbide-tipped metal-cutting blade. However, a dedicated metal-cutting saw like an angle grinder or cold saw is usually safer and more efficient for regular metal cutting.

**How do I make straight cuts with a circular saw?**
Use a straight edge or circular saw guide (fence) clamped to the workpiece. Set the blade depth to just below the thickness of the material — the blade should extend about 1/4 inch below the cut. Cut with the blade following the guide, keeping the saw base flat against the workpiece throughout.

**Is a 15-amp circular saw enough for homeowner use?**
Yes — 15 amps is the most common corded motor size and handles all standard homeowner tasks with ease. You would only need more power for industrial or continuous professional use.
