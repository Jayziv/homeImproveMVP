## Buyer's Guide: What to Look for in a Budget Cordless Drill

### Voltage (18V vs 20V vs 12V)
- **12V:** Light tasks, compact, great for finish work
- **18V/20V:** Standard for most home and DIY use — enough for decking, fencing, and furniture assembly
- Note: 18V (tool brand standard) and 20V MAX (DEWALT marketing) are functionally similar

### Battery Capacity (Ah)
- **1.3–1.5Ah:** Good for light occasional use
- **2.0Ah:** Better runtime for longer sessions
- **4.0Ah+:** Usually overkill under $100, but great if included

### Clutch Settings
More clutch positions = more torque control. Look for at least 15 positions. This prevents stripping screws and damaging surfaces.

### Brushed vs Brushless Motor
Brushless motors are more efficient, last longer, and provide better runtime. Increasingly available under $100. Worth prioritizing if you can.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What is the best cordless drill under $100?**
The DEWALT DCD771C2 is our top pick for most buyers. It combines brand reliability, 20V power, and two batteries at the sub-$100 price point.

**Is a 20V drill better than 18V?**
They are functionally equivalent. DEWALT uses "20V MAX" branding while other brands use "18V" — both refer to the same nominal voltage range.

**How long do budget cordless drill batteries last?**
Most 1.3–1.5Ah batteries will last 45–90 minutes of moderate use. Invest in an extra battery (often $20–30) if you plan extended sessions.

**Can I use a budget drill for drilling into concrete?**
No — standard drill/drivers are not designed for masonry. You need a hammer drill or rotary hammer. For occasional light masonry, a SDS-Plus rotary hammer is the right tool.
