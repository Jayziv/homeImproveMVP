## Buyer's Guide: Pressure Washer PSI & GPM Explained

### PSI (Pounds per Square Inch)
- **1,500–2,000 PSI:** Light duty — cars, patio furniture, light concrete
- **2,000–2,800 PSI:** Medium duty — driveways, decks, fences, siding
- **2,800+ PSI:** Heavy duty — heavily stained concrete, commercial use

### GPM (Gallons per Minute)
GPM matters as much as PSI. Higher GPM = faster cleaning. A 2,000 PSI / 2.0 GPM washer cleans faster than a 2,500 PSI / 1.1 GPM model.

**Cleaning Units (CU) = PSI × GPM**
Aim for 2,000+ CU for driveways.

### Electric vs Gas
| Feature | Electric | Gas |
|---|---|---|
| Noise | Quiet | Loud |
| Fumes | None | Yes |
| PSI | Up to ~2,500 | Up to 4,000+ |
| Maintenance | Minimal | Oil, spark plugs |
| Best for | Regular home use | Heavy grime, large areas |

### Nozzles
- **0° (red):** Concentrated — for tough stains, NOT for cars or wood
- **15° (yellow):** Paint stripping, heavy cleaning
- **25° (green):** General concrete and surface cleaning
- **40° (white):** Delicate surfaces, cars, windows
- **Soap (black):** Low pressure for applying detergent

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What PSI do I need to clean a concrete driveway?**
2,000–3,000 PSI is ideal for concrete. For lightly soiled driveways, 1,800 PSI works. For oil stains and heavy fouling, use 2,500+ PSI with a surface cleaner attachment.

**Can I use a pressure washer on asphalt?**
Yes, but use 1,200–1,500 PSI and a wide-angle nozzle. High pressure can damage asphalt surfaces.

**How often should I pressure wash my driveway?**
Once or twice per year is sufficient for most driveways. More frequently in wet climates where algae and moss accumulate.

**Do I need a surface cleaner attachment?**
For driveways, a surface cleaner attachment ($30–50) is highly recommended. It covers a wider area evenly and eliminates the streaking that wand-only washing causes.
