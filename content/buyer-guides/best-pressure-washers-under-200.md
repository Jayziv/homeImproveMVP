## Buyer Guide: What to Look For in a Budget Pressure Washer

### PSI and GPM: The Two Key Numbers
PSI (pounds per square inch) measures water pressure, while GPM (gallons per minute) measures flow rate. For effective cleaning, you need both. The cleaning power index (CU) is PSI multiplied by GPM — a higher CU means more cleaning power per pass. For driveways and decks, aim for at least 1,600 PSI and 1.2 GPM.

### Electric vs. Gas
All five models on this list are electric — and that is the right choice for most homeowners. Electric pressure washers are quieter, require no fuel or oil, emit no fumes, and are much easier to maintain. Gas pressure washers are more powerful but much more expensive and maintenance-intensive. For home driveways and decks, electric is ideal.

### Hose Length
Longer is better. A 20-foot hose restricts how far you can move from the outlet or water source. Look for 25-30 foot hoses, especially for large driveways. Extension hoses are available but can reduce pressure slightly.

### Nozzle Selection
Most budget pressure washers come with 3-5 quick-connect nozzles ranging from 0-degree (very concentrated) to 40-degree (wide fan) and a soap/low-pressure nozzle. A turbo nozzle rotates the water stream for extra cleaning power — the Ryobi RY141612 includes one.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I use a pressure washer to clean my car?**
Yes, but use the widest nozzle (40-degree) and keep the wand at least 12 inches from the surface to avoid paint damage. Never use a 0-degree nozzle on vehicle paint. The Greenworks GPW1501 is especially well-suited for car washing due to its lower pressure.

**How do I pressure wash a concrete driveway?**
Use a 25-degree nozzle and work in overlapping passes, keeping the nozzle 6-12 inches from the surface. Apply a concrete degreaser or detergent first for oil stains, let it dwell, then rinse. The Sun Joe SPX3000 and AR Blue Clean AR390SS have the power for thorough concrete cleaning.

**How often should I pressure wash my deck?**
Once a year in spring is ideal for most decks — to clear away winter grime and prepare the surface for staining or sealing. Always use a deck cleaner and a wide fan nozzle to avoid raising the wood grain.

**Are electric pressure washers powerful enough for driveways?**
Yes — models at 1,800 PSI and above handle most residential driveway cleaning effectively. For heavily stained or textured concrete, the AR Blue Clean AR390SS at 2,000 PSI will deliver the best results.
