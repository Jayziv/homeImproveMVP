## Buyer's Guide

### Rotary Hammer vs Hammer Drill
| Feature | Rotary Hammer | Hammer Drill |
|---|---|---|
| Mechanism | True piston-driven hammering | Ratchet/cam hammering |
| Impact energy | 1–8+ joules | < 1 joule |
| Best for | Concrete, masonry, demo | Light masonry, concrete block |
| Bit system | SDS-Plus or SDS-Max | Standard round/hex shank |

**For real concrete work, always use a rotary hammer — not a hammer drill.**

### SDS-Plus vs SDS-Max
- **SDS-Plus**: Most common for home/DIY — bits up to 1 inch, most accessories
- **SDS-Max**: For large demolition and bits 1 inch+ — contractor/professional use

### Impact Energy (Joules)
- **1–2 joules**: Light anchors, small holes in block/concrete
- **2–4 joules**: Standard home renovation — tile removal, anchor installation
- **4+ joules**: Heavy demolition, large-diameter holes
