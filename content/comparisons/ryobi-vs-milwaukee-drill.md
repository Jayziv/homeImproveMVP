## Head-to-Head Comparison

On paper, entry-level models from both brands look remarkably similar. However, Milwaukee's motor technology and build quality result in more consistent torque delivery under load. For drilling into hardwood, concrete anchors, or driving long screws repeatedly, Milwaukee pulls ahead. For standard household tasks, Ryobi performs admirably. **Winner: Milwaukee** — better sustained performance under heavy use, though Ryobi holds its own for light-to-medium tasks.

---

### Battery and Runtime

Ryobi's ONE+ batteries are competitively priced, and the platform's widespread availability means finding replacements or extras is easy and affordable. A 2Ah Ryobi battery typically costs $30-$40.

Milwaukee's M18 batteries are more expensive — a 2Ah pack runs $60-$80 — but they are engineered for faster discharge rates and include advanced cell technology that maintains voltage under high loads. The M18 REDLITHIUM batteries also have better cold-weather performance. **Winner: Milwaukee** — superior battery technology and runtime, though at a higher price per pack.

---

### Price and Value

This is where Ryobi shines. A Ryobi PCL206K1 drill and driver combo kit with battery and charger typically runs $80-$100. A comparable Milwaukee M18 kit costs $150-$250, and stepping up to the FUEL line pushes $300+.

For a homeowner who drills a few holes a month, paying three times more for a Milwaukee does not make economic sense. Ryobi delivers solid value for casual use. **Winner: Ryobi** — unbeatable value for the price, particularly for homeowners and light DIYers.

---

### Durability and Build Quality

Milwaukee drills are built to a noticeably higher standard. The housing is more robust, the chuck feels more precise, and the overall construction inspires more confidence for high-frequency use. Professional contractors regularly run Milwaukee drills for 8+ hours a day, year after year.

Ryobi drills are well-made for their price tier, but they are not designed for that level of abuse. Plastic components can wear over time with heavy use. For occasional home projects, Ryobi's durability is more than adequate. **Winner: Milwaukee** — clear edge in long-term durability and resistance to wear.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is Ryobi good enough for a homeowner?**
Absolutely. Ryobi ONE+ drills are well-made, reliable tools for typical home improvement tasks. If you are hanging pictures, assembling furniture, or doing occasional repairs, Ryobi gives you excellent performance without overspending.

**Are Ryobi and Milwaukee batteries compatible?**
No. Despite being owned by the same parent company (TTI), Ryobi and Milwaukee batteries are not cross-compatible. The platforms use different connectors and voltages.

**Is Milwaukee worth the extra cost?**
For professionals and serious DIYers who use their drills frequently, yes — Milwaukee's durability and performance justify the higher price. For light home use, the extra cost is harder to justify.

**Which drill lasts longer?**
Milwaukee drills are built to a higher standard and generally last longer under heavy use. Ryobi drills are durable for their price tier but may not hold up as well to daily professional use over many years.
