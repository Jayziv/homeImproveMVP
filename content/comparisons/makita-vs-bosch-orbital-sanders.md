## Orbit Size: What It Means

- **Makita BO5041 (3/32-inch orbit):** Smaller orbit = finer finish, less aggressive material removal. Better for final sanding passes and finish work.
- **Bosch ROS20VSC (1/8-inch orbit):** Larger orbit = faster material removal. Better for prep work, paint removal, and sanding rough lumber.

**Winner for fine finishing: Makita. Winner for fast material removal: Bosch.**

## Vibration

Bosch's dual-bearing counterbalance system is the single biggest differentiator. In independent vibration tests, the ROS20VSC produces 30–50% less operator vibration than the Makita BO5041 during sustained use.

For sanding sessions longer than 30 minutes, this matters enormously for hand and wrist fatigue.

**Winner: Bosch — by a significant margin.**

---

## Dust Collection

Both sanders offer port connectivity for shop vac hookup (where both excel). Comparing the standalone systems:

- **Makita**: Fabric dust bag — fills quickly, basic filtration
- **Bosch**: Microfilter dust canister — superior filtration, holds more dust

**Winner: Bosch**

---

## Power

Makita's 3.0A motor vs Bosch's 2.5A gives Makita a power edge, particularly noticeable when sanding hardwoods. Makita's wider variable speed range (4,000–12,000 OPM vs Bosch's 7,500–12,000) also allows much slower speeds for delicate finish work.

**Winner: Makita**
