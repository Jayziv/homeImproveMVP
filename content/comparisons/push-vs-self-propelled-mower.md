Buying a lawn mower seems straightforward until you realize how many variables matter: yard size, terrain slope, grass type, and physical stamina. The choice between a push mower and a self-propelled mower is one of the most important decisions you will make. Get it right and mowing is manageable; get it wrong and every mowing session becomes a workout.

---

## The Short Answer

- **Choose a push mower if:** Your lawn is small (under 1/4 acre), relatively flat, and you want a simpler, lighter, and less expensive machine.
- **Choose a self-propelled mower if:** Your lawn is larger than 1/4 acre, has slopes or hills, or you want less physical effort during mowing sessions.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is a self-propelled mower worth the extra cost?**
For lawns over 1/4 acre or any yard with slopes, yes — the reduced effort and time savings over years of mowing justify the higher price. For small flat lawns, a push mower is perfectly adequate and saves money.

**Is the Honda HRX217VKA hard to start?**
Honda's GCV200 engine is known for easy starting. The choke-free design means you typically just pull the cord and it starts. Proper fresh fuel and annual maintenance keep starting reliable year after year.

**Can push mowers handle hills?**
Push mowers can handle gentle slopes, but steep hills require significant effort. On grades over 15 degrees, a self-propelled mower becomes much more practical and safer to use.

**What lawn size requires a self-propelled mower?**
Most experts recommend considering self-propelled for lawns over 1/4 acre (about 10,000 square feet). Anything larger than 1/2 acre becomes genuinely tiring with a push mower over a full season.
