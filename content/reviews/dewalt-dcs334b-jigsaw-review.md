## Performance

### Wood Cutting
The DCS334B cuts through standard dimensional lumber (2×4, 2×6) smoothly at setting 3 orbital with full speed. A 10-foot straight cut across 3/4-inch plywood takes under 30 seconds with a coarse blade.

At orbital setting 0, with a fine-tooth T101B blade, it cuts clean, square edges in 3/4-inch hardwood plywood with minimal tear-out. Ready for finish joinery.

### Curves and Irregular Shapes
This is where jigsaws excel over circular saws. The DCS334B tracks curves smoothly — the tight 1-inch stroke keeps the blade responsive for tight radii. We cut a 4-inch radius circle in 1/2-inch oak with clean results on orbital setting 0.

### Metal Cutting
With a metal-cutting blade (T118A), the DCS334B cuts through 1/4-inch mild steel plate. Set speed to 1,000–1,500 SPM, orbital setting 0. Cuts cleanly but slowly — an angle grinder is faster for straight cuts in metal.

### Countertop Cutouts
One of the most common homeowner uses for a jigsaw. For sink cutouts in laminate countertops:
- Use a fine-tooth blade (80+ TPI)
- Orbital setting 0
- Tape the shoe to prevent scratching the laminate surface
- Score the cut line with a utility knife to minimize laminate tear-out
