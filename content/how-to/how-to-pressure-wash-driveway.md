# How to Pressure Wash a Driveway: Step-by-Step Guide

Pressure washing a driveway is one of the most satisfying home improvement tasks — it transforms a grey, stained surface back to near-new condition in an afternoon. This guide walks through the entire process, from choosing the right machine to finishing techniques.

---

## What You'll Need

### Equipment
- **Pressure washer** — 2,000–3,000 PSI for concrete ([see our top picks](../best-pressure-washers-home-driveways))
- **Surface cleaner attachment** (recommended) — $30–50, eliminates stripes
- **25° nozzle** (green) — for general concrete cleaning
- **0° nozzle** (red) — for stubborn stains (use carefully)
- **Soap nozzle** (black) — for applying degreaser

### Cleaning Supplies
- **Concrete degreaser** — for oil and grease stains
- **Concrete cleaner/detergent** — for general cleaning (many pressure washer-compatible)
- **Stiff bristle brush** — for pre-treating oil spots
- **Safety glasses**
- **Closed-toe shoes** (pressure washers can cause serious injuries)

---

## Step 1: Clear the Driveway

Remove all cars, bikes, furniture, and potted plants from the driveway. Move them far enough away to avoid overspray damage.

Sweep the driveway with a broom to remove loose dirt, leaves, and debris. This prevents clogging the surface cleaner attachment.

---

## Step 2: Protect Surrounding Areas

Pressure washers generate significant overspray. Before starting:

- **Cover nearby plants and grass** with plastic sheeting or tarps
- **Close all windows and doors** on the house side of the driveway
- **Move outdoor furniture** from adjacent patio areas
- **Alert neighbors** if the driveway is close to their property

---

## Step 3: Pre-Treat Oil and Grease Stains

Oil stains are the hardest part of driveway cleaning. Pressure alone won't remove embedded motor oil. Pre-treat stains 15–30 minutes before washing:

1. Apply a concrete degreaser generously to each oil spot
2. Scrub with a stiff bristle brush to work the degreaser into the stain
3. Let it soak for at least 15 minutes
4. Some stains require two applications — don't skip this step

## Step 4: Connect and Set Up the Pressure Washer

**For electric pressure washers:**
1. Connect the garden hose to the water inlet
2. Turn on the water supply fully
3. Attach the spray wand and high-pressure hose
4. Plug in and power on
5. Squeeze the trigger to purge air before attaching a nozzle

**For gas pressure washers:**
1. Check oil level before starting
2. Connect water supply and turn on fully
3. Prime the pump (consult manual)
4. Start the engine

**Set up the surface cleaner attachment** if you have one — attach it to the wand in place of the spray nozzle. This is the single biggest upgrade for driveway cleaning.

---

## Step 5: Apply Detergent (Optional but Recommended)

Before high-pressure washing, applying detergent loosens dirt and improves results:

1. Switch to the **soap/black nozzle** (low pressure)
2. Fill the pressure washer's detergent tank or use a downstream injector
3. Apply detergent in overlapping passes, working section by section
4. Let the detergent dwell for 3–5 minutes — don't let it dry
5. Switch back to the 25° green nozzle or surface cleaner for rinsing

---

## Step 6: Pressure Wash the Driveway

### Using a Surface Cleaner (Recommended)

A surface cleaner attachment is a rotating head that covers a 12–15 inch path uniformly. It's faster and eliminates the "tiger stripe" marks that wand-only washing causes.

1. Hold the surface cleaner flat against the concrete — don't lift it
2. Work in overlapping rows, like mowing a lawn
3. Move at a steady pace — don't pause or you'll leave circular marks
4. Overlap each row by 2–3 inches

### Using the Wand Only

If you don't have a surface cleaner:

1. Use the **25° (green) nozzle** for most surfaces
2. Hold the wand 10–12 inches from the concrete
3. Work in sweeping passes, moving left to right
4. **Always overlap passes by 50%** to avoid stripe marks
5. For stubborn spots, switch to the **15° (yellow) nozzle** — hold 12+ inches away

---

## Step 7: Treat Stubborn Stains

After the initial wash, inspect for remaining stains:

**Oil stains:** Apply another round of degreaser, scrub, let sit 15 minutes, then hit with the 25° nozzle at close range (6–8 inches) or carefully use the 0° red nozzle.

**Algae and mildew (green or black stains):**
- Apply a diluted bleach solution (1:10 bleach to water) using the soap nozzle
- Let dwell 5 minutes
- Rinse thoroughly with high pressure

**Rust stains:**
- Apply a rust remover (oxalic acid-based)
- Scrub and rinse
- Note: rust stains may not fully remove — they improve but some discoloration can remain

---

## Step 8: Final Rinse

After all sections are cleaned:

1. Do a final pass over the entire driveway with the 25° nozzle to rinse detergent and loosened debris to the street or storm drain
2. Pay extra attention to corners and edges where debris collects
3. Rinse toward the street/gutter, not toward the foundation or grass

---

## Step 9: Let It Dry

Allow the driveway to dry completely before driving on it — typically 2–4 hours in dry conditions. If you plan to seal the driveway (highly recommended), wait at least 24–48 hours for full drying.

## Tips for Best Results

- **Pressure wash in sections** — work from top to bottom, left to right
- **Never use the 0° red nozzle on concrete** unless you have a specific stain — it can etch the surface
- **Don't use bleach on colored/stamped concrete** — it can discolor
- **Wear safety glasses** — high-pressure water causes debris to ricochet
- **Do it annually** — annual cleaning prevents algae buildup and surface degradation

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How often should I pressure wash my driveway?**
Once a year is typically sufficient for maintenance. More often in climates with heavy algae, moss, or tree sap.

**Can I pressure wash an asphalt driveway?**
Yes — but use 1,200–1,500 PSI and a wide nozzle (40°). High pressure damages asphalt.

**Do I need a permit to pressure wash my driveway?**
No — but check local regulations about where the runoff goes. Many areas require runoff to drain to a permeable surface or storm drain, not directly into storm sewers without a filter.

**How long does driveway pressure washing take?**
A standard two-car concrete driveway takes 1–2 hours with a surface cleaner attachment, or 2–3 hours with wand only.
